## Abstract

The terrestrial atmosphere has traditionally been treated as a passive medium for mass and energy transfer. This whitepaper evaluates, from a strictly physical and biological perspective, the plausibility of considering the atmosphere as an active dielectric layer capable of electromagnetic coupling with living systems. The analysis integrates functional aerosols, the theoretical concept of foglets, and bioelectromagnetic resonance within the METFI (Toroidal Electromagnetic Internal Forcing Model of Earth) and the Theory of Learning by Exception (TAE). No regulatory narratives are invoked. Only experimentally grounded physics and biology are considered.
